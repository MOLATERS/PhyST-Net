\section{Case Studies}
\label{sec:case_studies}
To provide a deeper understanding of PhyST-Net's operational behavior, we conduct three qualitative case studies using the SDWPF and HoT datasets. These studies focus on temporal alignment during ramp events, spatial propagation of wind fronts, and the interpretability of future-ExG conditioning.

\subsection{Response to Rapid Wind-Ramp Events}
\label{sec:case_ramp}
Wind-ramp events, characterized by sudden increases or decreases in power over short intervals, pose a significant challenge for grid stability. Standard models often exhibit a "lag" in their response to these events due to the averaging effect of temporal aggregations. In this case study, we examine a 24-hour window from the SDWPF test set containing a significant upward ramp.

We attribute this to two factors: first, AGSP's soft-windowing preserves the high-frequency transitions that hard-patching might blur; second, K-AC's use of future exogenous forecasts allows the model to ``pre-adjust'' the latent state before the transition fully manifests in the historical target sequence.
